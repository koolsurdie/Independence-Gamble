The Independence Gamble

Lead Aureon Devices through a decade of market disruption

Your Company: Aureon Devices

Aureon Devices is a 140-year-old electronics manufacturer, recently spun off from its parent conglomerate. You've just been appointed CEO, and the company now stands on its own for the first time in decades.

You have a strong brand, a capable team, and serious competitors nipping at your heels. But the market is about to get a lot more turbulent.

Your Challenge

Across the next 10 years, supplier power, customer pressure, new entrants, substitutes, and competitive rivalry will keep changing. The economy may boom, stabilize, or fall into recession. Each year brings new threats and opportunities.

Your job: Make 20 strategic decisions (2 per year) that keep Aureon Devices not just surviving, but thriving. You'll make some decisions with incomplete information. You'll learn from consequences. And you'll discover that external forces matter more than you think.

How to Play

Years 1-3: You'll make decisions without seeing the market forces. After each decision, hidden forces are revealed. You'll learn what you should have anticipated.

Years 4-10: You'll see the market forces before deciding. Now you can apply what you learned. Strategy planning becomes real.

Each Round: Choose one strategic posture (Rapid Expansion, Steady Build, Fortress, or Pivot). Submit your decision. See the consequences. Debrief with your team and the trainer.

Your KPI Targets

💰 Cash Strength: 65/100+
📈 Market Share: 20/100+
⛓️ Supply Stability: 75/100+
🚀 Disruption Readiness: 70/100+

The Stakes

You have 20 major decisions to prove that Aureon can survive as an independent company and beat its competitors over time. If you grow too fast, you'll burn cash. If you protect cash too hard, you'll lose market share. If you ignore disruption, you won't have a business left to defend.

Your board wants results now. But the market will judge whether you're building a company that can last.

What You'll Learn

  • Porter's Five Forces: How external market forces constrain your choices
  • Scenario Planning: How to anticipate disruption before it hits
  • Double-loop Learning: How to learn from failures and adjust strategy
  • Strategic Thinking Under VUCA: How to decide when the future is uncertain
